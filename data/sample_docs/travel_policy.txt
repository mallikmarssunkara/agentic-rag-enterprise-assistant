=========================================
ACME ENTERPRISE - CORPORATE TRAVEL & EXPENSE POLICY
=========================================
Document ID: ACME-FIN-TRV-2026
Version: 6.3
Effective Date: February 1, 2026
Owner: Corporate Finance & Accounting Department

1. OBJECTIVE & BOOKING CHANNELS
-------------------------------
The objective of this policy is to ensure employees travel safely and comfortably while managing corporate expenses prudently. 
* Booking System: All business travel bookings (flights, hotels, rental cars) must be made through the corporate travel management portal, ACME Concur. Bookings made through consumer websites (e.g., Expedia, Booking.com, direct airline sites) are non-reimbursable unless pre-approved in writing by the Travel Coordinator due to unique cost advantages or emergency routing.
* Booking Advance: Travel plans should be finalized and booked at least fourteen (14) days prior to the departure date to secure early-bird fares. Bookings within 7 days of travel require manager sign-off.

2. AIR TRAVEL GUIDELINES
------------------------
* Domestic Flights (Under 6 Hours): Employees must book Standard Economy or Main Cabin seats. Basic Economy is discouraged due to change restrictions. Comfort+/Premium Economy seats require department manager approval.
* Mid-Range Flights (6 to 8 Hours): Premium Economy seats are fully reimbursable.
* Long-Haul International Flights (Over 8 Hours): Business Class travel is permitted, subject to written pre-approval from the division Vice President (VP) and the CFO.
* First Class: First Class tickets are strictly non-reimbursable under any circumstances.
* Frequent Flyer Miles: Employees may retain airline loyalty miles and points for personal use; however, flight choices must be based on the lowest logical fare, not personal program preferences.

3. ACCOMMODATION STIPENDS & LIMITATIONS
---------------------------------------
* Standard Travel Locations: Hotel lodging reimbursement is capped at 200 USD per night (excluding taxes and occupancy fees).
* High-Cost (Tier-1) Locations: For major metropolitan hubs, the nightly cap is adjusted up to 350 USD per night. Approved Tier-1 cities include:
  - North America: New York City (NYC), San Francisco (SF), Los Angeles (LA), Boston, Chicago, Washington D.C., Toronto.
  - Europe: London, Paris, Geneva, Zurich, Frankfurt.
  - Asia-Pacific: Tokyo, Singapore, Hong Kong, Sydney.
* Exceeding Caps: If a suitable hotel cannot be secured within these caps, employees must obtain written pre-approval from their direct supervisor and the Travel Coordinator before completing the booking.

4. MEALS, INCIDENTALS & ENTERTAINMENT
-------------------------------------
* Standard Meal Allowance: Daily meal and incidental expenses (M&IE) are reimbursed based on actual costs up to 60 USD per day (including tips).
* Tier-1 Location Meal Allowance: Daily allowance is increased to 90 USD per day.
* Receipts Requirement: Itemized receipts are mandatory for all individual expenses exceeding 25 USD. Credit card transaction slips alone are not sufficient.
* Business Entertainment: Expenses incurred while entertaining clients or business partners must include:
  - Names of all attendees and their company affiliations.
  - The business purpose of the meeting.
  - Approval by the host's direct manager.
  - Alcohol limits: Reimbursed up to two (2) drinks per attendee, provided it is part of a business meal. Excess alcohol is not reimbursable.

5. GROUND TRANSPORTATION & RENTAL CARS
--------------------------------------
* Preferred Options: Public transit, airport shuttles, and standard ride-sharing (UberX, Lyft standard) are the preferred methods of ground transportation. Black car or premium ride services are not reimbursable.
* Rental Cars: Allowed only when public transit or ride-share options are impractical.
  - Car Class: Reimbursement is limited to Intermediate, Compact, or Economy class.
  - Insurance: Employees must decline the rental company's optional collision damage waiver (CDW) and personal accident insurance, as ACME's corporate credit card provides comprehensive insurance coverage.
  - Fuel: Rental vehicles must be returned with a full tank of fuel. Fuel service fees charged by rental agencies are non-reimbursable.
* Personal Vehicle Mileage: If using a personal vehicle for business travel, mileage is reimbursed at the IRS standard rate of 0.67 USD per mile (for 2026). Normal daily commuting distance must be deducted from the total trip mileage.

6. EXPENSE REPORT SUBMISSION
----------------------------
* Submission Window: Expense reports must be submitted via ACME Concur within thirty (30) days of the trip's completion.
* Late Submissions: Expense reports submitted after sixty (60) days require division head approval, and those submitted after ninety (90) days will be rejected, resulting in non-reimbursement.
